What is Diabetic Retinopathy?

Diabetic Retinopathy is a complication of diabetes that affects the eyes. It‚Äôs caused by damage to the blood vessels of the retina. It may start with no symptoms or mild vision problems, but can eventually cause blindness.

Causes of Diabetic Retinopathy

It is caused by high blood sugar levels damaging the small blood vessels in the retina. Risk factors include long-term diabetes, high blood pressure, cholesterol, and smoking.

Possible Treatments

Treatments include controlling blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol. Advanced treatments involve laser therapy, anti-VEGF injections, or vitrectomy surgery depending on severity.
